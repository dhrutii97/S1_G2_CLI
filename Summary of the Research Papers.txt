 Summary of the Research Papers
-In the research on Multi-Scale Predictability of High-Impact Weather in the Battlespace Environment, it is determined that unpredictability in weather prediction of extreme weather conditions is due to both initial condition errors and model physics errors.
-To overcome this, the authors apply ensemble forecasting, in which a combination of model runs in which the conditions produce a probability distribution of the potential results but not a single forecast. 
One of the fundamental innovations is the addition of stochastic physics, by adding random variations to convective parameterizations, to model uncertainty. This greatly enhances the spread of ensembles in areas of deep convection and enhances prediction of tracks and wind extremes of tropical cyclones. 
-The paper demonstrates that the relationship between spread and skill is stronger, which is that ensemble variability is a more valid predictor of real forecast error. A Bayesian Approach uses a Bayesian classifier to predict the probability of rainfall occurrence based on the meteorological variables of pressure, humidity, temperature, and wind speed.
-The model calculates the probability of events of rainfall using conditional probabilities instead of giving deterministic results. Nevertheless, it is based on historical data trends and does not explicitly model the physical atmospheric dynamics and hence it might not be able to perform with extreme events that have never been experienced.
-The authors present the probabilistic optimization methods like Cross-Entropy methods, natural evolution strategies, and Gaussian process-based Bayesian optimization. These are effective in determining optimal predictor weights to enhance reliability of the ensemble at a considerably lower cost of computation. More accurate uncertainty estimates and better probabilistic predictions are obtained using better tuned ensembles.
